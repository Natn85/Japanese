# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Japanese learners at every stage, from complete beginners to intermediate and advanced students. Primary context: self-study, often mobile, motivated but needing encouragement to push through the grind of memorization. The job to be done is making progress feel visible and achievable.

## Product Purpose

Japanara is a structured Japanese learning platform covering hiragana, katakana, kanji, grammar, and vocabulary via flashcards and guided lessons. Success looks like a learner who completes each module feeling capable and moving forward.

## Brand Personality

Friendly, encouraging, capable. The tone is a knowledgeable friend, not a classroom. Celebrates small wins. Never condescending, never cold.

## Anti-references

- Duolingo: gamified to the point of feeling hollow, progress bars everywhere, cartoon style
- Generic "SaaS dark mode" apps: cold neutral palette, no personality, design clearly secondary
- Overly minimal/"serious" study tools that feel punishing rather than inviting

## Design Principles

1. **Progress is the product.** Every screen should answer "how am I doing?" Make advancement visible without over-gamifying it.
2. **Clarity over decoration.** The content (kana, kanji, grammar) is visually dense. Chrome should step back and let the characters breathe.
3. **Encouragement without condescension.** Warm copy and motion that celebrate effort, not just correctness.
4. **One primary action per screen.** Reduce decision fatigue for learners who are already working hard.
5. **Cultural authenticity.** Japanese characters are a design element, not a decoration. Use them with intention.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. All interactive elements keyboard-navigable. Reduced motion support required. Text contrast ≥ 4.5:1. Touch targets ≥ 44×44px on mobile.
